Welcome!

I am an undergraduate Computer Science student at Georgia Tech where I work as a research assistant advised by Polo Chau and a senior teaching assistant for CS 1332: Data Structures and Algorithms taught by Mary Hudachek-Buswell.

As a member in the research group, the Poloclub of Data Science, I build interactive data visualization tools for machine learning. I am interested in continuing research in explainability and interpretability of deep learning architectures.

As a senior teaching assistant for the Data Structures and Algorithms course in the College of Computing, I develop all of the homework assignments for the course taught in Java, which ranges from writing grading JUnits to conducting weekly homework reviews for other TAs to critique the assignments.

Over the past two summers, I have worked as a software engineer at Google , working on core search ads and play store teams.

In my freetime, I love running (add me on Strava!), basketball, music, and trying to salvage my rapidly deteriorating piano skills.
